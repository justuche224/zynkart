Scroll through Instagram or X (Twitter) in Nigeria, and you will see it everywhere. A beautiful product photo, an engaging caption, and at the very end, the dreaded phrase: **"DM for Price."**

Many vendors believe that forcing a direct message creates an opportunity to "sell" to the customer privately. Some do it to hide their pricing from competitors. Others do it out of sheer habit.

But the data is clear: **"DM for price" is costing you sales.**

Here is why you need a DM for price alternative, and how it can instantly boost your revenue.

## The Psychology of "DM for Price"

When a potential customer sees a product they like, their interest is at its peak. They want to know one thing: *Can I afford this?*

By forcing them to DM you, you are introducing massive friction into the buying process. 

1.  **The Introvert Tax:** Many buyers simply do not want to talk to you. They want to browse, buy, and leave.
2.  **The Waiting Game:** If you take 3 hours to reply, their excitement has died. They have likely already bought from a competitor who listed their price publicly.
3.  **The Embarrassment Factor:** Customers hate asking for a price, finding out they can't afford it, and then having to awkwardly say "Okay, I'll get back to you." They would rather just not ask.

## You Are Not Protecting Yourself from Competitors

The most common defense of "DM for price" is dodging competitors. Here is the harsh truth: Your competitors can just use a fake account to DM you and get your price. You are only succeeding in hiding your price from the people actually trying to give you money.

## The Ultimate "DM for Price" Alternative

The solution is not just pasting your price in the caption—although that is a good start. The true alternative is giving your customers a frictionless, self-service shopping experience.

You need a storefront.
